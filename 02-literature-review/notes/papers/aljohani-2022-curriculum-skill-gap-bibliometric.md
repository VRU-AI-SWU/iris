## Research Question
What are the dominant research themes, key contributors, and intellectual structure of the literature on curriculum alignment and skill gap between higher education and the job market (2010–2021)?

## Limitations of Existing Methods
Prior reviews of curriculum-job market alignment relied on manual, narrative literature reviews with limited scope. No comprehensive, data-driven mapping of the field existed. Cross-disciplinary connections between research groups were invisible without co-authorship and co-citation network analysis.

## Contribution
First bibliometric analysis of the curriculum alignment / skill gap literature field. Mapped 10,214 Scopus records (2010–2021) using co-authorship networks, citation networks, and keyword co-occurrence networks. Identified dominant themes, top institutions, and temporal evolution of the field.

## Proposed Method
Bibliometric analysis on 10,214 cross-disciplinary Scopus records. Keywords searched: "curriculum alignment", "acquired skills", "required skills". Analyses: publication/citation trends, co-authorship networks, keyword co-occurrence networks. Tools: VOSviewer or similar bibliometric software.

## Key Findings
- Field grew substantially 2010–2021; computing/IT is the most researched domain for curriculum-job alignment
- Dominant themes: ontological skill classification, semantic-based knowledge representation for courses, job posting analysis, competency modelling
- Keyword co-occurrence clusters: (1) semantic web/ontology, (2) NLP/text mining for job postings, (3) higher education policy, (4) Industry 4.0 / digital transformation
- Most active regions: USA, UK, Saudi Arabia, China
- Growing use of job posting data (rather than surveys) as the primary market signal — a methodological shift in the field post-2015
- Gap identified: lack of longitudinal studies tracking whether curriculum revisions reduce observed gaps

## Limitations of This Paper
Bibliometric analysis describes the literature structure, not the quality or validity of specific methods. Scopus coverage skews toward Western/English-language publications — Thai and Southeast Asian work is substantially under-represented. Does not propose or validate any skill extraction or gap quantification method.

## Notes for Iris
Useful as background context: confirms that the curriculum-to-market gap field is mature in Western literature but has a methodological blind spot for Southeast Asia / non-English markets. The dominance of ontology-based and NLP approaches validates our design choices. The identified shift from surveys to job-posting-based analysis (post-2015) is the same shift our work continues. Low operational relevance to Iris design decisions — treat as field map, not methodological input.
